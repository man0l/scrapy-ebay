87 of 95 people found the following review helpful
Very convenient to use.
By Amazon Customer on December 14, 2013
Color: 1 coil-black Verified Purchase
I have a Nexus 5 and 7 and am very pleased with the convenience of using wireless charging. Two comments:

- Charging seems to take about 40-50% longer than with the usual 2-Amp USB charger. This is not generally a problem unless you are in a hurry. The wireless charger is so convenient to use. You can easily put it on the charger and take it off when needed.

- But you REALLY need to CENTER the device exactly over the charger. I have a Kill-A-Watt power meter and used it to test how much power was being used. With the Nexus perfectly centered it consumed 8 to 9 watts. Move it off center by a quarter inch and it dropped to about 6 watts. At only a half inch off center and it drops to less than 1 watt -- essentially not charging.

With nothing on the pad it used only 0.3 watts, necessary to detect a device placed on it.

Note that the overall efficiency of the USB charger/Wireless pad/Nexus power flow is probably below 50%. The 120 VAC to 5V USB charger efficiency is probably less than 80%. The Pad's 5V DC conversion to the 150 kHz magnetic field output (by the pad's primary coil) is probably less than 70% efficient. The magnetic coupling efficiency to the Nexus (secondary coil) is very much subject to positioning as mentioned above. Nonetheless, it works very well and is reliable and easy to use.

This pad is much thinner than the one offered from Google. A mini USB cable is supplied for your own charger.
